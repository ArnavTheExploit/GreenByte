E-Waste Awareness

What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. Used electronics which are destined for reuse, resale, salvage, recycling, or disposal are also considered e-waste.

Environmental Impact

Improper disposal of e-waste releases toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium into the soil and groundwater, harming ecosystems and wildlife.

Health Risks

Exposure to e-waste toxins can cause serious health issues in humans, including respiratory problems, DNA damage, and reproductive issues.

Do's and Don'ts

✅ Do's

  • Donate functional electronics to charity.
  • Take e-waste to certified recycling centers.
  • Backup your data before disposing of devices.

❌ Don'ts

  • Do not throw electronics in regular trash bins.
  • Do not dismantle devices yourself (batteries can explode).
  • Do not incinerate e-waste.
